# Example b1
<hr style="height:4px;border:0;background:#4a90e2;">



<br/>

This example duplicates the [geant4 basic example B1][example-B1]. It uses the `dosimeter` digitization plugin 
to store the accumulated dose on the detector.

<br/>

## Geometry

The world is a box containing another box made of water.

The water envelope includes two volumes: a spherical cone and a trapezoid. The two inner volumes
are made from tissue and bone materials, common for medical applications and pre-defined in the 
Geant4 Material Database.

- G4_A-150_TISSUE
- G4_BONE_COMPACT_ICRU

## Generator

As in the Geant4 example, the default kinematics is a 6 MeV gamma, randomly distributed in front of the envelope. 
This can be customized in the command line, or in the yaml file:

```yaml

gparticle:
  - name: gamma
    p: 6
    vz: -15
    delta_vx: 4
    delta_vy: 4
    multiplicity: 1
```

<br/>

## Digitization

The `Shape2` volume is associated to the `dosimeter` digitization in `geometry.py`:

```python
        gvolume.digitization = 'dosimeter'     
        gvolume.set_identifier('box', 2)
```

As a result the variable `dose` is accumulated throughout events in a run using the formula:

$$
\mathrm{dose} = \frac{\mathrm{etot}}{\mathrm{mass}}
$$

where `etot` is the total energy deposited in each event and `mass` is the mass of `Shape2`.
